Ideal Job

My ideal job in IT is structured and formulaic. Where if I know what to do and how to do it, I
just need to apply critical thinking and problem-solving skills to complete my task and
then go home. A job as a software tester intrigues me for that reason. I would clock in,
gather data, set up tests, and troubleshoot until the sun comes down just to do it the
next day. In this IT job, the main tasks are to oversee new software, test it, and make
sure it works. I would gather and analyze data, create test plans, construct both
manual and automatic tests (for UI, logs, and databases), assist others, give tips on how
to troubleshoot their work, and of course troubleshoot as well.


This job interests me as I am looking for a more passive role in IT as someone who sits at the
back helping others while still being part of a team effort. One of this job’s roles is to
be both comfortable in group tasks and solo work. I think I would be exemplary in this
environment as I would be able to help others in a group while still having my own
space to work alone. Another skill requirement is a critical and detailed mindset. I tend
to take things slow and methodical making sure everything is right to a tee. This can
sometimes be a detriment. However, I think in this work environment it would be a
great strength.


The job requires a qualification in computer science or a degree in a relevant field. A
qualification that can only come with time and a lot of effort. I’ll also need two or more
years of experience in Linux and Windows UI testing. They also ask for relevant
experience in JIRA (a proprietary issue tracking), GIT (a software system that tracks
changes in any set of files allowing programmers to collaboratively work on projects),
and SVN (an open-source version control system). Finally, familiarity in languages such
as Python, Bash, and MySQL is essential.


If I want to have this job in the future, I will need to work hard to understand a variety of
programming languages and become acquainted with the many programs in the IT
world such as GIT and JIRA. Also, I will need relevant work experience and a degree in
computer science or a related field. All things come in time, but you have to start
somewhere so hopefully this introductory course into IT will lead me down
a long and fulfilling path to new knowledge and future possibilities.
